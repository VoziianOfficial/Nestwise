Company story

Built around one simple problem: scattered provider searches.

Many homeowners first notice wildlife activity through small signs: scratching sounds in the attic, droppings near exterior areas, movement near the roofline, or possible entry points around vents and soffits. The next step can feel unclear because each provider may ask for different information.

Nestwise was designed to make that first step more organized. Instead of presenting itself as a wildlife service company, Nestwise focuses on request matching. Homeowners can describe the concern, choose a relevant category, and compare independent provider options when available in their area.

The platform keeps its role clear: Nestwise helps with the connection and request structure. The actual work, pricing, inspection, recommendations, and service decisions belong to independent providers and the homeowner.

What Nestwise does

Helps homeowners compare provider options with better context.

  • Organizes wildlife-related requests by concern type.
  • Helps homeowners share useful details like ZIP code, sounds, signs, and timing.
  • Supports matching with independent local providers when available.
  • Reminds homeowners to verify licenses, insurance, qualifications, and scope.
What Nestwise does not do

Does not act as the wildlife contractor.

  • Does not perform wildlife removal services directly.
  • Does not employ technicians or claim to inspect homes.
  • Does not guarantee provider work, pricing, timing, or results.
  • Does not replace homeowner verification before hiring.

How the platform helps

Better details can lead to better provider conversations.

Wildlife concerns can involve different service types depending on the issue, location, property layout, species, local rules, and provider qualifications. Nestwise helps homeowners start with details that may be useful for provider review.

Sounds

Where activity is heard and when it happens.

Signs

Droppings, odor, damage, nesting material, or exterior marks.

Access

Possible gaps, vents, soffits, rooflines, chimneys, or attic areas.

Location

ZIP code and service area information for provider availability.

Important homeowner note

Final provider choice belongs to the homeowner.

Nestwise can help create a clearer starting point, but homeowners should independently review each provider before hiring. Confirm licenses, insurance, qualifications, local wildlife requirements, written estimates, inspection fees, service scope, and any work guarantees offered by the provider directly.
