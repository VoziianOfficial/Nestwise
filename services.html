What to compare

Provider fit is more than a quick price.

Wildlife-related requests can involve inspection, exclusion, repairs, cleanup, seasonal timing, or local legal requirements. Homeowners should compare the full service scope before choosing a provider.

Written service scope

Ask what is included in the estimate and whether inspection, prevention, cleanup, or repairs are separate.

Licenses and insurance

Homeowners should verify provider qualifications, coverage, and requirements for the work being performed.

Local availability

Provider coverage, scheduling, pricing, and service options can vary by ZIP code and wildlife concern.

Clear estimates

Compare estimate format, inspection fees, follow-up visit details, and communication before making a decision.

Common request details

Helpful details to include when requesting matching.

Sounds

When and where activity is heard

Mention if sounds happen at night, early morning, near the attic, wall, roofline, vent, chimney, or crawl area.

Signs

Visible evidence around the home

Droppings, disturbed insulation, odor, stains, chewing marks, feathers, nesting material, or damaged vents may help providers understand the concern.

Access

Possible entry points

Describe gaps, loose vents, soffit openings, roofline access, damaged screens, or areas where animals may be entering.

Photos

Safe visual context

If safe, photos of exterior openings or visible signs can help providers review the request before discussing next steps.

Verification

Questions before hiring

Ask about licensing, insurance, written estimates, local wildlife rules, inspection process, and service scope.
